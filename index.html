Scams & Horror Stories

#1 "Phantom Damage" Scam
Agent points out pre-existing scratch/dent at return, demands $200-500 "repair fee." ALWAYS record a 360-degree walkaround video with the attendant in the frame before leaving the lot.
Fake Website Scam
Clone websites for "Caye Caulker Rentals" demand full payment via Zelle/Venmo. Legit companies take credit card or payment on arrival. NEVER pay via Zelle/Venmo.
Gas/Fuel Gouging
Cart given at 1/4 tank, told "return as found." Then charged $50 "refueling fee" for $5 of gas. Insist on full-to-full policy. Photograph gas gauge at pickup AND return.
C&N Rental Agreement Violation
Customer paid 11 days, cart damaged day 5. C&N demanded $700 for repairs (independent shop: $300). Company ghosted. No resolution.
Price Bait-and-Switch
Quoted $90 verbally, billed $136 because "you had the cart on the last day." Get total price in writing via WhatsApp BEFORE arrival. Screenshot the message.
Deposit Not Returned
Cash deposits harder to recover. Use credit card hold instead. Ensure return inspection happens while you are present.

On the Island

CUK airstrip is on the south end. Your accommodation at 133 Xocol is in the village center — a $5 golf cart taxi ride or ~10 min walk. If you've pre-booked, your rental company will meet you at the airstrip with the cart.

No cars on the island. Only golf carts, bicycles, and feet. Three streets run north-south. Sand roads, no pavement. Impossible to get lost.

Do You Need a Taxi from the Airstrip?

No Taxi Needed

If you pre-book your golf cart via WhatsApp, the rental company delivers it to the CUK airstrip and meets you when you land. You drive straight to 133 Xocol (~5 min). No taxi required.

If you arrive WITHOUT a cart pre-booked: golf cart taxis wait at the airstrip. Flat rate $5 USD ($10 BZD) anywhere on the island. Or walk ~10 min to 133 Xocol.
